Privacy Policy for PhotoSync

Effective Date: [June 2025]

PhotoSync is a Python application that allows users to upload photos from a local directory on their personal device to a private album in their own Google Photos account. This Privacy Policy outlines how the application handles user data.

1. Information Accessed

PhotoSync only accesses the following:

  • Local files (photos) in a directory you specify

  • Your Google Photos account, with your explicit consent, for the following purposes:

    • Retrieving your personal albums

    • Uploading new photos

    • Creating a new album, if specified

2. How Your Data Is Used

All accessed data is used exclusively to perform the photo upload and synchronization process. PhotoSync does not collect, store, analyze, or transmit your data to any third-party service or external server.

  • No user data is logged or stored outside your device.

  • No background data collection occurs.

  • All API interactions are performed directly between your system and Google Photos via secure HTTPS connections.

3. Data Sharing and Storage

  • No third-party sharing: Your information is never shared with any external service, person, or organization.

  • No cloud storage: PhotoSync does not upload your files anywhere except your authenticated Google Photos account.

  • No analytics or telemetry: The app contains no tracking code or analytics tools.

4. Authentication and Security

  • Authentication is handled securely via Google OAuth 2.0.

  • Access tokens are stored locally and securely, typically in your system's user directory (e.g., token.json or similar).

  • You may revoke app access at any time via your Google Account permissions.

5. Intended Use

PhotoSync is intended for personal use by individuals with a technical background. Users run the application themselves and have full control over its execution and environment.

6. Your Consent

By using PhotoSync, you consent to the application accessing your local files and Google Photos account only for the purposes described above.
